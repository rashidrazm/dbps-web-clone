6 Ways to Raise Funds for Scaling Your Business in India

  1. Bootstrapping
  2. Crowdfunding
  3. Bank Loans
  4. Venture Capital
  5. Angel Investors
  6. Government Schemes

1. Bootstrapping

The first option for a small company would be to bootstrap, which means self-funding the business. This can be done by reinvesting profits, borrowing from friends and family, or using personal savings.

2. Crowdfunding

Crowdfunding platforms like Kickstarter, Indiegogo, and Ketto can help small companies raise funds by pitching their business idea to a large number of people who are willing to contribute small amounts of money.

3. Bank Loans

Small companies can approach banks for loans to fund their business expansion. However, the approval of bank loans depends on the creditworthiness of the company and its ability to repay the loan.

4. Venture Capital

Small companies can also raise money from venture capitalists who are interested in investing in promising businesses. Venture capitalists invest in a company in exchange for a share of ownership and a percentage of the profits.

5. Angel Investors

Angel investors are high-net-worth individuals who invest in startups and small businesses. In India, several angel investor networks such as Indian Angel Network, Mumbai Angels, and Hyderabad Angels can help small businesses raise funds.

6. Government Schemes

The Government of India offers several schemes and programs to support small businesses. Companies can explore these schemes to see if they are eligible for funding.
